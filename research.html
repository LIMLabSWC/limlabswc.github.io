Research

Understanding the fundamental principles of learning, inference, and memory

Research Questions

Statistical Learning and Pattern Recognition

Finding and exploiting patterns and regularities in the environment is a critical brain function for animals living in a complex yet structured world. Individuals can deduce abstract relations, and learn these regularities through experience, and use this prior information to guide future behaviour. The process of inferring statistical patterns and priors constitutes the foundation of further cognitive abilities.

Key Questions:

  • How do animals infer these patterns and relations?
  • How are priors formed, represented, and stored?
  • What are the neural mechanisms underlying statistical learning?

Memory Organization and Integration

Our past, present, and future are intimately linked by our memories of various timescales. The brain is constantly engaged in storing new memories and executing actions while integrating incoming sensory input with past memories and internal models of the world.

Key Questions:

  • What are the fundamental principles of (sensory) memory organization?
  • How are memories utilized in inference problems?
  • How are learned priors integrated in memory, or used in combination with new sensory information?

Our Approach

At LIM Lab we employ a synergistic combination of theory and experiment to tackle the fundamental principles of neuronal computations underlying sensory memory organization and sensory inference. We use high-throughput training to combine sophisticated, well-controlled and quantifiable behavioral paradigms with powerful tools to monitor and manipulate neural circuits.

In all of our research programs, experiments are intertwined with hypotheses drawn from theoretical investigations and computational models.

Theory

Computational models and theoretical frameworks guide our experimental design and interpretation.

Experiment

Sophisticated behavioral paradigms with precise control and quantification.

High-throughput Training

Efficient training protocols for rapid data collection and analysis.

Neural Monitoring

Advanced tools to monitor and manipulate neural circuits in real-time.

Research Areas

Human Psychophysics

We study human perception and decision-making using carefully controlled psychophysical experiments to understand how humans learn patterns and make inferences about the world.

Animal Behavior

Our research with rats and mice allows us to investigate the neural mechanisms underlying learning and memory at the cellular and circuit level.

Computational Modeling

We develop theoretical frameworks and computational models to understand the principles governing learning, inference, and memory formation.
